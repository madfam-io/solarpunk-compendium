# Announcing The Solarpunk Almanac

What would it look like to create practical infrastructure for the solarpunk movement?

Not just fiction or aesthetics ‚Äî though those matter ‚Äî but actual tools, guides, directories, and community structures that help people build regenerative futures in their own communities?

That's what we're building.

## The Vision

The Solarpunk Almanac aims to become open infrastructure for the global solarpunk movement: an educational hub, coordination layer, and resource commons that helps millions learn, connect, and act.

We inherit a lineage of practical wisdom publications:
- The Farmer's Almanac (1818‚Äìpresent)
- Poor Richard's Almanack (Benjamin Franklin)
- The Whole Earth Catalog (Stewart Brand)
- The Foxfire Books

And we extend this tradition into a solarpunk future ‚Äî aligned with natural rhythms, powered by community contribution, and freely accessible to all.

## What We're Building

**Seasonal Editions**
Quarterly guides released at solstices and equinoxes. Each edition contains practical knowledge for the season ahead: growing guides, preservation techniques, energy efficiency tips, community organizing strategies, and more.

**Global Directory**
A searchable database of solarpunk-aligned projects worldwide. Find ecovillages, community gardens, makerspaces, repair cafes, tool libraries, and organizations near you ‚Äî or discover inspiring projects across the globe.

**Practical Tools**
Not just articles, but actual tools: carbon footprint calculators, seed starting planners, solar sizing guides, community asset mapping templates, and more. Knowledge made actionable.

**Community Network**
Local chapters building regenerative futures together. A framework for starting and sustaining solarpunk communities wherever you are.

## Our Values

We believe in:
- **Pragmatic optimism** ‚Äî Hope as strategy, not naivety
- **Ecological harmony** ‚Äî Humans as part of nature, not separate
- **Radical inclusivity** ‚Äî Futures built by and for everyone
- **Community sovereignty** ‚Äî Tools, not top-down solutions
- **Open knowledge** ‚Äî Strengthening the commons

## Join Us

## Content Calendar Framework

### Weekly Themes
- **Monday:** Project Spotlight (from global directory)
- **Tuesday:** Practical Tips (how-to content)
- **Wednesday:** Community Feature (chapter highlights)
- **Thursday:** Resource Share (tools, books, films)
- **Friday:** Future Visions (inspiration, solarpunk art)
- **Weekend:** Community engagement, Q&A

### Seasonal Content
- **Equinox/Solstice:** New edition announcements
- **Monthly:** Season-specific practical content
- **Quarterly:** Community roundups, impact reports

## Visual Guidelines

### Colors
- **Solar Gold:** #F59E0B (primary accent)
- **Leaf Green:** #10B981 (secondary accent)
- **Solarpunk Teal:** #14B8A6 (tertiary)
- **Deep Forest:** #1F2937 (dark backgrounds)
- **Warm White:** #FFFBEB (light backgrounds)

### Typography
- **Headlines:** Orbitron (futuristic, technical)
- **Body:** Inter (clean, readable)

### Image Style
- Nature integrated with technology
- Community gatherings and collaboration
- Gardens, solar panels, green architecture
- Warm, hopeful lighting
- Diverse people and places
- Avoid: sterile tech, dystopian imagery, individual heroism

## Engagement Guidelines

### Response Tone
- Be helpful and warm
- Acknowledge different contexts and constraints
- Avoid perfectionism or judgment
- Celebrate small steps
- Connect people with resources

### Community Building
- Amplify community content (repost with credit)
- Ask questions, invite participation
- Highlight diverse voices
- Create space for local knowledge
- Build relationships, not just followers

- [ ] Secure social handles on all platforms

- [ ] Write and schedule announcement posts

- [ ] Monitor and respond throughout day
