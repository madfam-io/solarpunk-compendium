# Contributing to The Solarpunk Almanac

Thank you for your interest in contributing to The Solarpunk Almanac! This project is built by and for the community, and we welcome contributions of all kinds.

---

## Ways to Contribute

### Content Contributions

- **Articles and Guides** — Practical how-tos, case studies, tutorials
- **Translations** — Help us reach more communities by translating content
- **Project Submissions** — Add your project to our global directory
- **Resource Recommendations** — Share books, films, podcasts, tools
- **Photography and Art** — Visual content that embodies solarpunk aesthetics
- **Local Knowledge** — Bioregional wisdom, traditional practices, indigenous knowledge

### Technical Contributions

- **Code** — Platform development, bug fixes, new features
- **Design** — UI/UX improvements, illustrations, icons
- **Data** — Help us build and maintain our databases
- **Infrastructure** — DevOps, accessibility audits, performance optimization

### Community Contributions

- **Start a Chapter** — Organize locally in your region
- **Host Events** — Workshops, skill-shares, film screenings
- **Mentorship** — Guide newcomers to solarpunk practice
- **Moderation** — Help maintain healthy community spaces

---

## Contribution Guidelines

### Code of Conduct

All contributors must adhere to our values:

1. **Be respectful** — Disagree constructively, assume good faith
2. **Be inclusive** — Welcome diverse perspectives and experiences
3. **Be patient** — Not everyone has the same knowledge or background
4. **Be collaborative** — We're building together, not competing

### Content Standards

- **Accuracy** — Fact-check your contributions, cite sources
- **Accessibility** — Write clearly, use alt text, consider all readers
- **Originality** — Only submit work you have the right to share
- **Relevance** — Align with solarpunk values and our mission

### Technical Standards

- **Code quality** — Follow existing patterns, write tests, document
- **Commits** — Clear commit messages describing the change
- **Pull requests** — Describe what and why, link related issues
- **Review** — Be open to feedback, iterate constructively

## Getting Started

### For Content Contributors

1. **Read our Mission** — Understand our values and goals (docs/MISSION.md)
2. **Explore existing content** — See what we already have
3. **Identify gaps** — What's missing that you could add?
4. **Submit your contribution** — Via our submission form or GitHub issue

### For Technical Contributors

1. **Fork the repository**
2. **Set up your development environment** (see README)
3. **Find an issue** — Look for "good first issue" labels
4. **Make your changes** — On a feature branch
5. **Submit a pull request** — With clear description
6. **Respond to review** — Iterate as needed

### For Community Organizers

1. **Read our Chapter Toolkit** (coming soon)
2. **Connect with us** — Let us know you're interested
3. **Find local allies** — Who shares these values?
4. **Start small** — A single event can grow into a movement
